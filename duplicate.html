  1. On the night of March 21, 1991, the LAPD stopped a motorist for a traffic violation. After resisting police orders, King was beaten with nightsticks and kicked by the police. The incident was videotaped and brought to the Grand Jury a week after. On April 29,1992 the trial verdict announced that the indicted officers were acquitted of the majority of their criminal charges. The verdict resulted in explosive riots beginning the next night in Los Angeles. In many of the outbreaks, rioters pulled motorists out of their cars and attacked them in South Central Los Angeles.




  2. In 2009, a BART police officer shot and killed 22 year old Oscar Grant on a platform at Fruitvale station on New Years Day. Grant and several other Black male passengers were pulled off of the train and detained on the platform. The police officer claimed that he was going to use his taser, but accidentally reached for his gun and shot Grant in the back.
